How This Calculator Works

  1. Self-employment tax: Your net income × 92.35% × 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare). Social Security is capped at $184,500 (2026, per SSA).
  2. Half-SE deduction: You deduct 50% of your SE tax from your income before calculating income tax.
  3. QBI deduction: If eligible, you deduct up to 20% of net business income (subject to income limits).
  4. Federal income tax: Applied to your income after the standard deduction ($16,100 single / $32,200 joint), half-SE deduction, and QBI deduction, using 2026 progressive brackets.
  5. State income tax: Applied using your state’s 2026 rates and standard deduction.
  6. Above-the-line deductions: Health insurance, retirement contributions (SEP IRA / Solo 401k), home office (simplified method: $5/sq ft, max 300 sq ft), and student loan interest (max $2,500) reduce your income before tax brackets are applied.
  7. Child Tax Credit: $2,200 per qualifying child under 17 (increased from $2,000 by OBBBA), applied as a credit against federal income tax (not a deduction). Phases out at $50 per $1,000 of income above $200,000 (single) / $400,000 (joint).
  8. Quarterly estimated tax: Total tax divided by 4. Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15.
